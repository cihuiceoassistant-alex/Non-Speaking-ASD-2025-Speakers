Abstract (摘要)

• Frederick Shic 教授是一位傑出的學者，他的使命是將技術與臨床洞察力相結合，打造下一代工具，以了解和改善患有自閉症和其他發展障礙兒童的生活。

Professor Robert Pennington

Title

• Professor and William T. Bryan Endowed Chair in Technology at the University of Kentucky.

• Director of the Center of Excellence on Inclusive Practice at the Ohio Center for Autism and Low Incidence (OCALI).

Education

• Ph.D. in Special Education from the University of Kentucky.

Expertise

• Core Focus: Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA), emphasizing evidence-based practices (EBPs) for students with disabilities.

• Researching systematic instruction, such as using response prompting and frames, to teach complex written expression skills (e.g., sentence construction, writing about text).

• Investigating the effectiveness of autonomous technology-based instructional programs for teaching writing.

• Developing and evaluating strategies like Functional Communication Training (FCT) to address challenging behavior and build communicative competence.

• Utilizing innovative technologies, including social robots, to facilitate social skills and peer interactions for children with ASD.

• Professional Development: Focused on improving the quality of teacher preparation, including co-developing the Autism and Low Incidence Classroom Observation Tool (ALCOT) and promoting High Leverage Practices.

Certification and Award

• Certification: Board Certified Behavior Analyst, Doctoral Level (BCBA-D).

• Licenses: Holds Kentucky Teaching Certificates for Moderate to Severe Disabilities (K-12) and Elementary Education (K-4).

• Selected Honors: Recipient of the Felix S. Barker Award, the KYABA Innovator in Behavior Analysis Award, and the UL President's Distinguished Faculty Award.

Research Funding and Leadership

• He actively drives professional development initiatives, having directed federally funded projects totaling over $9.5 million, aimed at integrating high-quality, evidence-based behavioral and instructional practices into inclusive school environments.

Abstract

• Dr. Pennington is a prominent special education researcher specializing in Applied Behavior Analysis and the needs of students with ASD and Extensive Support Needs (ESN).

• His research has demonstrated the efficacy of using systematic instruction and technology to build crucial academic skills, particularly written expression (e.g., sentence construction and paragraph writing).

Chia-Kai Chang (Fei-Man Teacher)

Chia-Kai Chang (also known as Fei-Man Teacher) is an educator focused on special education and art integration, renowned for his outstanding contributions in guiding special students through art elements such as fine arts and music. He has received the Ministry of Education's Excellent Special Education Personnel Award and promotes the "Fei-Man Philosophy" supportive counseling, helping students build learning motivation and life goals through art and interests.

Main Features and Contributions

* Integration of Art and Special Education: Believes art can attract special education students with low learning motivation, incorporating art into the curriculum to enhance student focus and quality of life.

* "Fei-Man Philosophy": Advocates slow living and quality learning and life, guiding students to explore interests through "waste utilization" and "slow" creation processes.

* Diverse Teaching: Designs innovative special education art courses, organizes teacher workshops, provides individualized learning strategies, and focuses on the artistic and cultural functions of school libraries.

* Counseling and Support: Provides psychological counseling and support groups to help special students find life goals and passions.

* Awards: Recipient of the Ministry of Education National Excellent Special Education Personnel Award and New Taipei Art Education Contribution Award.
